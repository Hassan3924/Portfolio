Vessel Schedule Optimization

Notice: Due to confidentiality agreements, code is not publicly available. Happy to discuss methodology and results in detail upon request.

Overview

Developed a genetic algorithm to optimize container vessel routing and scheduling for global liner services. The model minimizes total voyage cost by prioritizing fuel consumption while reducing transit time, subject to strict operational constraints including exact weekly port calls and realistic container handling times at each stop.

The resulting schedules achieved robust fuel efficiency and perfect schedule reliability, demonstrating the practical value of evolutionary optimization techniques in maritime logistics. The tool provides a flexible framework for evaluating trade-offs between cost, speed, and service frequency in real-world shipping networks going through 1000s of combination that meets the requirement of the company while fulfilling the targets (e.g. time (date and port), fuel efficiency, schedule optimization, etc)

Key features

  • Enforced rotation cycles divisible by a number of days to guarantee consistent timely departures between vessels.
  • Incorporated vessel-specific fuel consumption curves for accurate cost modeling.
  • Produced complete schedules with departure and arrival times for every port call.
  • Analyzed port call distribution to identify and report potential congestion patterns.

Challenges

  • Vessel balancing where too many vessels arrive on the same port.
  • Making sure to strike a balance between fuel and time.

Results

  • Optimal scheduling for vessels throughout the next quarter. 
  • Balanced fuel consumption while traveling to each leg.
  • Optimized speed for each leg a vessel should travel (for different types of vessel).
